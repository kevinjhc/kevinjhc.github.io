Product Design Process

1. Understand and align on goals

Methods:

  • Understand business, product, technical goals
  • Stakeholder map exercise
  • Risks and mitigations exercise

2. Research and understand the user’s needs

Methods:

  • Exploratory interviews
  • Proto-personas
  • Day-in-the-life / user scenarios

3. Brainstorm how to solve the user’s needs

Methods:

  • Design studio
  • Create wireframes and prototypes

4. Validate whether solution concepts meet users' needs

Methods:

  • Low-fidelity UI
  • Test prototypes and get user feedback

5. Identify MVP to start building product

Methods:

  • High-fidelity UI
  • Beta release plans
  • Assumptions tracker

6. Build and release product iteratively

Methods:

  • Continue to meet with users
  • Increase design fidelity by creating a style guide

Principles

Balanced Teams

Product decisions are most effective when made by a balanced team of designers, product managers, and engineers.

User Centered Design

Product teams should be in close contact with users to ensure that valuable software is being built.

Lean Product Development

Products are risky because they are built on assumptions — features should be built incrementally and released early and often to ensure that these assumptions are being validated by actual users.

Continuous Improvement

Product teams should apply tight feedback loops not only to the products they work on, but also towards improving their teams. Regular retrospectives and actionable, specific, kind feedback help build trust on a team.
