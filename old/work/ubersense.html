Design Details: Mobile Dropdown

Overview

The iOS7 launch popularized the slide-to-reveal more actions interaction. I found that even though it was a popular interaction, it confused our target users. I learned that "best practices" should be understood within the context of my target users.

My Role

  • Visual and interaction design
  • Iterating on designs based off user feedback

OVERVIEW

I worked on features touching all parts of the Ubersense iOS and Android apps and web video player. While redesigning how users interact with videos, we adopted the swipe-to-left interaction pattern. However, backlash from our users helped us reevaluate our design decision and quickly change to a dropdown to reduce confusion.

Key takeaway: "Established" design patterns are helpful because they make certain interactions easier to understand. However, they may not be appropriate depending on your users. Through the iteration process, it is important to discern which ones are helpful for your users.

Context

One of the main screens a user interacts with is the “My Videos” screen. This screen displays all of a user’s videos and the most important metadata: Date, Video Tags, and whether the video is local or stored in the cloud.

From this screen videos can be viewed, searched, shared, and edited. It would be overwhelming to the user to provide all of the actions upfront.

Swipe-to-left Interaction

We ended up applying the swipe-to-left interaction to show the actions right around when iOS7 was just released. Even though it was a new pattern, we thought it was prevalent enough because it was adopted in Apple's iOS Mail app.

A more familiar pattern: the dropdown

We showed the interaction to several users and received positive feedback. However, when we released it into the wild, we received an inordinate amount of backlash - many coaches were confused.

  • We misunderstood our target audience. Many were older and slower to adopt the latest design patterns.
  • We put an informational graphic in onboarding, but it wasn't understood well enough of skipped.

We listened to our users and shortly after, I designed the dropdown menu.
